RA - ACR20 Response

(Efficacy to Week 24-52)

Study Design

RAPID-1 was a 52-week, phase III, multi-centre, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial with 982 RA patients randomised to Cimzia® 400mg + MTX at 0, 2, and 4 weeks and thereafter 200mg Q2W or 400mg Q2W# + MTX or placebo + MTX (2:2:1). Co-primary endpoints were the ACR20 response at week 24 and the mTSS at week 52. Results reported as percentage of patients achieving ACR response.1

#Dosage not currently registered in Australia.

ASAS40 Response

(Efficacy to Week 24)

AS - ASAS40 Response

(Efficacy to Week 24)

Study Design

RAPID-axSpA# was a multi-centre, randomised, double blind placebo controlled study in 325 patients ≥18 years of age with adult onset axial SpA for at least 3 months. The majority of patients had active AS (n=178). Patients had active disease activity as defined by the BASDAI ≥4 and spinal pain ≥4 on a 0-10 NRS. Patients must have been intolerant to or had inadequate response to ≥1 NSAID. Patients were treated with Cimzia® 400mg 0, 2 and 4 weeks and thereafter 200mg Q2W or 400mg Q4W or with placebo (1:1:1). The primary outcome was ASAS20 response at week 12.1

#Cimzia® is indicated for the treatment of adults with moderate to severe active RA, active AS and active PsA.

References

1. Landewé R, Braun J, Deodhar A et al. Ann Rheum Dis 2014; 73 (1): 39-47.

ACR20 Response

(Efficacy to Week 24)

PsA - ACR20 Response

(Efficacy to Week 24)

Study Design

RAPID-PsA was a multi-centre study in 409 patients with adult-onset PsA of at least 6 months' duration who had failed ≥1 DMARD (non-biologic or biologic). It was double-blind, placebo-controlled to week 24; dose-blind to week 48; then open label to week 216. Patients were treated with Cimzia® 400mg 0, 2 and 4 weeks and thereafter 200 mg Q2W or 400 mg Q4W or with placebo (1:1:1). 19.8% of patients on Cimzia (combined doses) and 19.1% on placebo had previous exposure to ≥1 anti-TNF. Primary endpoints were ACR20 response at week 12 and mTSS change from baseline at week 24# (#co-primary endpoint not met).1,2

References

1. Mease PJ, Fleischmann R, Deodhar AA et al. Ann Rheum Dis 2014; 73 (1): 48-55.

RA - ACR20 Response

(Prior Anti-TNF Use to Week 12)

Study Design

REALISTIC was a 12-week, double-blind period of a phase III study examining the efficacy and safety of Cimzia® in 1,063 adult RA patients with an unsatisfactory response or intolerance to at least 1 non-biological DMARD. Patients may have received up to two previous anti-TNFs and were stratified by baseline MTX use, previous anti-TNF therapy and disease duration. Primary endpoint was ACR20 response at week 12.1

Reference

1. Weinblatt ME, Fleischmann R, Huizinga TWJ et al. Rheumatol 2012; 51: 2204-2214.
